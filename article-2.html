Algerian Deglet Nour Sees Record European Demand in 2025 Season

Premium Algerian date varieties are capturing premium shelf space in French and German retail chains — and ACM is at the centre of this export surge.

Each export season tells you something about how a market is changing, and the 2025 season has told a particularly encouraging story for Algerian agriculture. Deglet Nour — long regarded as the "queen of dates" for its honey-like flesh and delicate translucency — is moving through European retail at volumes ACM's export desk hasn't seen in years.

Why Demand Is Climbing

A few forces are converging at once. European consumers are increasingly drawn to naturally sweetened, minimally processed foods, and dates fit that shift well. At the same time, French and German retail chains have been actively diversifying their premium produce sections, looking for products with a clear origin story and distinctive quality — exactly the positioning Algerian Deglet Nour has built over decades.

ACM's role in this has been to ensure that the supply chain behind the product is as reliable as the product itself: consistent grading, proper cold-chain logistics, and documentation that meets European import standards without friction.

"Quality alone doesn't win shelf space. Reliability does. Retailers need to know that what arrives in March will be the same as what arrived in October."

What This Means for Local Producers

Increased export demand has a direct effect further up the chain — for the growers and cooperatives ACM works with across the date-producing regions of Algeria. As volumes grow, so does the incentive to invest in better post-harvest handling, sorting technology, and packaging, all of which raise the baseline quality of what reaches international markets.

  • Higher grower margins as export-grade product commands a premium over local-market sales.
  • Investment in processing infrastructure at the cooperative level, improving consistency season over season.
  • Stronger negotiating position for Algerian exporters as demand outpaces some competing origins.

Looking Ahead

ACM expects this momentum to continue into the next season, and is working with partner farms to expand cultivated area without compromising the quality standards that built this demand in the first place. The goal isn't just to sell more — it's to make sure Algerian dates remain associated with the quality that earned them this position.
