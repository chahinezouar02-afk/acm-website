ACM Launches Water-Efficient Farming Initiative Across Three Algerian Provinces

A new drip-irrigation consulting programme launched in partnership with regional agricultural authorities, targeting 5,000 hectares by 2026.

Water is the single most contested resource in Algerian agriculture, and how it's used will largely determine which farms thrive over the next decade. In April 2025, ACM launched a new consulting initiative aimed directly at this challenge — bringing modern drip-irrigation systems and water-management consulting to partner farms across three provinces.

Why Drip Irrigation, and Why Now

Traditional flood and furrow irrigation methods, while deeply familiar to local farming practice, lose a significant portion of water to evaporation and runoff before it ever reaches a plant's roots. Drip irrigation delivers water directly to the root zone, drop by drop, on a schedule tuned to each crop's actual needs.

The technology itself isn't new globally — but deploying it well, on existing farms with existing crop patterns and existing economics, requires consulting expertise that goes beyond simply installing pipes. That's where ACM's role centers.

"The hardware is the easy part. The hard part is designing a system around how a specific farm already works, so adoption doesn't disrupt the season."

The Scope of the Programme

The initiative is structured in three phases across the participating provinces:

  • Assessment — soil and water-table analysis on each partner farm to design a system suited to its specific conditions.
  • Installation & training — drip systems installed alongside hands-on training for farm operators, ensuring the technology is actually used correctly.
  • Monitoring — ongoing consulting support through the first full growing cycle, adjusting the system as real-world data comes in.

ACM's target is to bring 5,000 hectares under this water-efficient model by 2026, in close partnership with regional agricultural authorities who share the goal of building more resilient farming practices across the region.

A Long-Term Bet on Sustainability

This initiative reflects something central to how ACM approaches consulting: sustainability isn't a separate concern from productivity, it's the foundation of it. A farm that runs out of water has no yield to speak of. By investing in water efficiency now, partner farms are building the kind of resilience that will matter most in the seasons ahead.
